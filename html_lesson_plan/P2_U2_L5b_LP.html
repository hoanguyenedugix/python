    • Activity B: Look and write
    15
    421
    Stage aims
    To reinforce understanding and use of “what” questions for the verb “do” by having students look at pictures and complete sentences with the correct form. This stage helps develop accuracy in writing and strengthens vocabulary recall.
    Context
    Stage 2: Preparing the list
    • Say to Ss: “OK, I have the list. Oh, but I don’t know if I’ve got a … or an… at home. Can you help me check? This is the list of food I have at home.”
    • Show a list of food body on the board, check the suggested body from the list.
    • Say to Ss: “OK! I have some things, but I don’t have everything… and I don’t know what he/wants… what can I do?!”
    • TA says: “You can ask them!!”
    • Say to Ss: “That’s a good idea!” Mime making a phone call and repeat back the words to the Ss e.g. “Oh, you want a … and a…”
    Heads up
    Focus
    • Draw Ss attention to the CPT.
    Presentation
    • Ask Ss to look at question 1, are you writing one or two words? (one)
    • Ask Ss to look at question 2, are you writing one or two words? (two)
    • Ask: “Is it ‘do you’ or ‘does you?” (do you)
    Demonstration
    • Demonstrate the activity with a co-teacher/student.
    Heads down
    Instruction
    • Use gestures. Say “Look and write”.
    ICQs
    • Ask: “Are we going to talk?” (no)
    • Ask: “Are we going to write neatly?” (yes)

    • Monitor. Provide support/stretch if needed.

    • Activity C: Unscramble and match
    15
    421
    Stage aims
    To develop writing and sentence-building skills by having students unscramble a sentence, write the words in the correct order and match pictures to the questions. This stage reinforces correct spelling, grammar, and question-answer structures in a structured way.
    Context
    Stage 3: Wrapping Up & Reflection
    • Say to Ss: “OK everyone! This is great. Here’s the list of food I have at home, here’s the list of things from you, and here’s the list of things my friend wants…”
    • TA says: “Let’s check the lists together”
    • Everyone helps to find matches on the list.
    • Say to Ss: “Well done everyone! You are amazing. OK… I think I still need to go shopping! Wish me luck with my dinner!”
    Heads up
    Focus
    • Draw Ss attention to the CPT.
    Presentation
    • Point to each sentence, referring back to the question structure in the grammar box.
    • Point to each picture in turn, trace the line back to the sentence.
    • Focus Ss on how many lines there are.
    • Ask Ss where they can find the answers (grammar box).
    Demonstration
    • Demonstrate the activity with a co-teacher/student.
    Heads down
    Instruction
    • Use gestures. Say: “Unscramble and match.”
    ICQs

    • Ask: Are we going to write neatly? (yes)

    • Monitor. Provide support/stretch if needed.

    • Phonics: Page 4, Activity A and B
    20
    490
    Stage aims
    To develop phonics awareness and fine motor skills by having students draw lines, trace and say words with /ch/ sounds, to reinforce recognition. This stage helps strengthen pronunciation, spelling, and reading skills in an engaging way.
    Context
    Stage 1: Setting the Scene
    • Say to the Ss: “Can you tell me some words with ch and sh sounds?” (write as many words on the board as you can)
    • Say to TA: “OK! Let’s play a game. I say a sound, you circle the words with that sound” (demonstrate with an example)
    • “Are you ready? Let’s go!” (pick sounds including ones from the lesson)
    • Say to Ss: “Can you pick a sound? And I’ll find the words! Ready! Go!”
    Heads up: Teacher presents the language or activity
    Focus
    • Draw Ss attention to the CPT.
    Presentation
    • Ask: “What pictures are on the left?” (dish, sheep, chip, cheese)
    • Ask: “How many pictures are there?” (eight)
    Demonstration
    • Demonstrate the activity with a co-teacher/student.
    Heads down: A: Feed the monster ch words. Draw lines.
    Instruction
    • Ask Ss to open books. [Student Book 2A page 114] Countdown from 5 to keep time.
    • Use gestures. Say: “Feed the monster ch words. Draw lines”
    ICQs
    • Ask: Are you going to write? (no)
    Activity and Monitor
    • Monitor. Provide support/stretch if needed.
    Feedback
    • Review pronunciation, correct mistakes together.

    • Phonics: Page 5 and 6, Activity A, B, C and D
    20
    491
    Stage aims
    To develop phonics, reading, and speaking skills by having students point and say words with /ch/ and /sh/, listen and read a story, circle words from the story. This stage reinforces sound recognition, comprehension, and spoken fluency in a meaningful context.
    Context
    Stage 2: Listening & Noticing the Sounds
    • Say to the TA: “Let’s play a game… You tell me some letters… and I’ll make a word! What do you think?” (can use letters more than once)
    • (TA writes ‘c, h, s, h, e, i, p, f, k’ and maybe one or two more)
    • TA says: “OK, you have 30 seconds, how many words can you make?”
    • Ss help countdown the timer and count the number of words the Teacher writes.
    Heads up
    Focus
    • Draw Ss attention to the CPT.
    Presentation
    • Ask Ss what they can see in picture 1 and 2 in turn.
    Demonstration
    • Demonstrate the activity with a co-teacher/student.
    Heads down: A: Point and say.
    Instruction
    • Ask Ss to open books [Student Book 2A page 115]. Countdown from 5 to keep time.
    • Say words and sentences describing each picture in turn, while Ss point.
    • Use gestures. Say: “Point and say.”
    ICQs
    • Ask: “Are you going to point?” (yes)
    Activity and Monitor
    • Monitor. Provide support/stretch if needed.
    Feedback
    • Review understanding, correct mistakes together.
    Heads down: B: Listen and read.
    Instruction
    • Use gestures. Say: “Listen and read.”
    ICQs
    • Ask: “Are you going to listen?” (yes)
    Activity and Monitor
    • Monitor. Provide support/stretch if needed.
    Feedback
    • Review pronunciation, correct mistakes together.
    Heads down: C: Choose words from the story and write.
    Instruction
    • Ask Ss to get their pencils. Countdown from 5 to keep time.
    • Use gestures. Say: “Choose words from the story and write.”
    ICQs
    • Ask: “What words will you write?” (shoes, chip, fish, lunch)
    Activity and Monitor
    • Monitor. Provide support/stretch if needed.
    Feedback
    • Review writing, correct mistakes together.
    Heads down: D: Say the story with your friends.
    Instruction
    • Demonstrate with the TA, read the text under the picture first. TA reads the speech bubbles. Swap roles at the end and read again.
    • Use gestures. Say: “Say the story with your friends..”
    ICQs
    • Ask: “Are you going to speak?” (yes)
    • Ask: “Are you going to take turns?” (yes)
    Activity and Monitor
    • Monitor. Provide support/stretch if needed.
    Feedback
    • Invite open pairs to act out the story in front of the class.
    Reward moment
  • Centre yourself, settle the class.
  • Check behaviour expectations.
  • Reward/remind and award points.
