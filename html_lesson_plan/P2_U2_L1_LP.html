    Stage aims
    To help students learn and remember new classroom-related words (marker pen, stapler, paintbrush, glue stick, colouring pencil, pencil sharpener) by listening, pointing to the correct picture, and saying the words. This stage helps students understand the vocabulary before using it in later activities.
    Context
    Stage 1: Setting the Scene
    • Say to the TA/Ss: “Oh no! I’ve lost my pencil case! Can you help me find it?”
    • Walk around and look at the Ss pencil cases: “What’s in your pencil case? Hmm it’s nice, but it’s not mine!”
    • Say: “Aha! Here it is, let’s look inside. I’ve got a … and my favourite….!”
    • Say to the students: “Amy, Nick, Rosa, Tim and Minh like these things too! Do you want to see? Let’s go!”
    Heads up: Teacher presents the language or activity
    Focus
    • Draw Ss attention to the CPT.
    Presentation
    Meaning
    • Point to body and say the words.
    Form
    • Focus Ss on the beginning, and end sounds.
    Pronunciation
    • Drill words (class, groups, individual).
    CCQs
    • Ask: “What has Tim got?” (colouring pencil)
    • Ask: “What has Amy got?” (marker pens)
    • Ask: “What colour is the glue stick?” (orange and blue)
    Demonstration
    • Demonstrate the activity with a co-teacher/student.
    Heads down: Students complete book activities
    Instruction
    • Ask Ss to take books and pencils out of their bags.
    • Ask Ss to open books [Student Book 2A page 13]. Countdown from 5 to keep time.
    • Ask: “What colour is the glue stick?” (orange and blue)
    ICQs
    • Ask: “Are we going to listen?” (yes)
    • Ask: “Are we going to point or write?” (point)
    Activity and Monitor
    • Play the audio. Pause if necessary. Play again.
    • Monitor. Provide support/stretch if needed.
    Feedback
    • Review pronunciation, correct mistakes together.

    • Activity C: Ask and answer
    15
    391
    Stage aims
    To introduce and practice saying sentences with “What have you got?” and “I’ve got a/an…” by using pictures and asking questions. This stage helps students use the target language naturally before moving on to more structured activities.
    Context
    Stage 4: Checking your shopping basket
    • Act out taking things out and putting things back in your pencil case (not only classroom objects, small toys or other known vocabulary is good too).
    • Say to the TA and students: “I have some things in my pencil case, do you want to see?”
    • Mime taking out one object at a time and asking: “Have I got a…?”
    • Guide students to use the target language (e.g., “Yes, you’ve got a… / No, you’ve got a …”).
    • Encourage students to do the same to you and TA with their pencil cases.
    Heads up: Teacher presents the language or activity
    Focus
    • Draw Ss attention to the CPT.
    Presentation
    Meaning
    • Look at each picture in turn asking questions.
    Form
    • Briefly demonstrate the contraction ‘have got’ = ‘‘ve got’ using 2 fingers that then come together.
    Pronunciation
    • Drill sentences focusing on ‘’ve got’ (class, groups, individual).
    CCQs
    • Ask: “Number 1, what have you got?” (I’ve got a marker pen)
    • Ask: “Number 2, what have you got?” (I’ve got a paintbrush)
    Demonstration
    • Demonstrate the activity with a co-teacher/student.
    Heads down
    Instruction
    • Ask Ss to open books [Student Book 2A page 14]. Countdown from 5 to keep time.
    • Use gestures. Say: “Ask and answer.”
    ICQs
    • Ask: “Are you going to speak or write?” (speak)
    Activity and Monitor
    • Monitor. Provide support/stretch if needed.
    Feedback
    • Review pronunciation, correct mistakes together.

    Stage aims
    To give students practice in asking and answering questions using “What has he/she got?” with “He’s/ She’s got…” in a structured activity. This stage helps build confidence in using the target language in conversations.
    Context
    Stage 5: Choosing what to buy
    • All the students need to choose only two things.
    • Say to the TA: “Look at all the things. OK, let’s choose two each. Can you help me?”
    • Mime holding up an object, saying the name and TA nods/shakes head or says Yes/No.
    • Repeat to the students.
    • Say: “OK! I’ve got the… and the … Now you choose two things! Ready?”
    • Say: “Have you got your favourite things? Well done everyone!”
    Heads up: Teacher presents the language or activity
    Focus
    • Draw Ss attention to the CPT.
    Presentation
    Meaning
    • Ask Ss to look at what’s in the bags. Say: “What can you see?”
    Form
    • Draw Ss attention to the sentence structure for ‘What has he/she got?’ and ‘He’s/ She’s got a …”
    Pronunciation
    • Drill questions (class, groups, individual).
    CCQs
    • Ask: “Do we say - What have she got? Or what has she got?” (What has she got?)
    • Ask: “Do we say - she’ve got a… ? Or she’s got a… ” (She’s got a …)
    Demonstration
    • Demonstrate the activity with a co-teacher/student.
    Heads down
    Instruction
    • Tell Ss to get ready to listen.
    • Use gestures. Say: “Ask and answer”.
    ICQs
    • Ask: “Are you going to speak or write?” (speak)
    • Ask: “Are you going to talk to your partner?” (yes)

    • Monitor. Provide support/stretch if needed.

    • Chant: What have you got?

    Stage aims
    To help students practice and remember the target language (“What have you got? I’ve got … What has he/she got? He/she’s got …”) through a fun and repetitive chant. This stage reinforces pronunciation, rhythm, and confidence in using the language naturally.
    Context
    Stage 6: Celebration & Wrap-Up
    • Say to Ss: “Great job, everyone! Now that we have everything, let’s celebrate our teamwork with a chant!”
    • Teach a fun classroom-themed chant using the new vocabulary and sentence structures.
    • To close the lesson, say: “Time to go home! Let’s sing our chant one last time”
    • Praise students for their teamwork and effort: “Well done, Team!”
    Heads up: Teacher presents the language or activity
    Focus
    • Draw Ss attention to the CPT.
    • Say, let’s watch and read.
    Presentation
    Skimming
    • Ask: “What are we going to chant about?” (classroom objects)
    Scanning
    • Ask: “In the chant, what has she got?” (pencil sharpener/ colouring pencil)
    Comprehension
    • Ask: “How many classroom words are there?”
    • Ask: “Can you point to the glue stick?”
    Demonstration
    • Demonstrate the activity with a co-teacher/student.
    Heads down
    Instruction
    Instruction
    • Ask Ss to open books [Student Book 2A page 47]. Use gestures for Ss to tap a finger on the word to follow the chant. Say: “Read and chant”
    ICQs
