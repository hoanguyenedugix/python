Topic: Activity A: Listen, point and say
15
Title: Stage aims
Summary: To help students learn and remember new classroom-related words (marker pen, stapler, paintbrush, glue stick, colouring pencil, pencil sharpener) by listening, pointing to the correct picture, and saying the words. This stage helps students understand the vocabulary before using it in later activities.
Title: Context

Sections:

Section: Stage 1: Setting the Scene
Details:
  • Say to the TA/Ss: “Oh no! I’ve lost my pencil case! Can you help me find it?”
  • Walk around and look at the Ss pencil cases: “What’s in your pencil case? Hmm it’s nice, but it’s not mine!”
  • Say: “Aha! Here it is, let’s look inside. I’ve got a … and my favourite….!”
  • Say to the students: “Amy, Nick, Rosa, Tim and Minh like these things too! Do you want to see? Let’s go!”

Topic: Activity B: Listen and circle
15
Title: Stage aims
Summary: To help students recognize and understand classroom-related words (marker pen, stapler, paintbrush, glue stick, colouring pencil, pencil sharpener) by listening and circling the correct word or picture. This stage checks their understanding of the vocabulary before they use it in speaking or writing activities.
Title: Context

Sections:

Section: Stage 2: Discovering the problem
Details:
  • Model the listening text with the TA, use any example except ‘paintbrush’.
  • Say and mime to the TA: “I’m going to draw something… can you guess what it is?” (draw any simple picture with some colours - leave it out one colour)
  • TA replies: “It looks great!”
  • Say: “Thank you! But, I haven’t got a blue marker pen”
  • TA: “I’ve got one, here you are!”
  • Say: “Thank you very much! Ah… that’s better. Do you want to listen to Nick and Amy? OK, let’s go!”

Topic: Activity C: Ask and answer
15
Title: Stage aims
Summary: To introduce and practice saying sentences with “What have you got?” and “I’ve got a/an…” by using pictures and asking questions. This stage helps students use the target language naturally before moving on to more structured activities.
Title: Context

Sections:

Section: Stage 4: Checking your shopping basket
Details:
  • Act out taking things out and putting things back in your pencil case (not only classroom objects, small toys or other known vocabulary is good too).
  • Say to the TA and students: “I have some things in my pencil case, do you want to see?”
  • Mime taking out one object at a time and asking: “Have I got a…?”
  • Guide students to use the target language (e.g., “Yes, you’ve got a… / No, you’ve got a …”).
  • Encourage students to do the same to you and TA with their pencil cases.

Topic: Activity D: Ask and answer
15
Title: Stage aims
Summary: To give students practice in asking and answering questions using “What has he/she got?” with “He’s/ She’s got…” in a structured activity. This stage helps build confidence in using the target language in conversations.
Title: Context

Sections:

Section: Stage 5: Choosing what to buy
Details:
  • All the students need to choose only two things.
  • Say to the TA: “Look at all the things. OK, let’s choose two each. Can you help me?”
  • Mime holding up an object, saying the name and TA nods/shakes head or says Yes/No.
  • Repeat to the students.
  • Say: “OK! I’ve got the… and the … Now you choose two things! Ready?”
  • Say: “Have you got your favourite things? Well done everyone!”

Topic: Chant: What have you got?
15
Title: Stage aims
Summary: To help students practice and remember the target language (“What have you got? I’ve got … What has he/she got? He/she’s got …”) through a fun and repetitive chant. This stage reinforces pronunciation, rhythm, and confidence in using the language naturally.
Title: Context

Sections:

Section: Stage 6: Celebration & Wrap-Up
Details:
  • Say to Ss: “Great job, everyone! Now that we have everything, let’s celebrate our teamwork with a chant!”
  • Teach a fun classroom-themed chant using the new vocabulary and sentence structures.
  • To close the lesson, say: “Time to go home! Let’s sing our chant one last time”
  • Praise students for their teamwork and effort: “Well done, Team!”
