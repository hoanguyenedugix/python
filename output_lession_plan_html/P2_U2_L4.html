Topic: Activity A: Find the eight hidden pictures
20
Title: Stage aims
Summary: To consolidate learning by having students complete a writing and a listening exercise using the key vocabulary, and practise the key structures by playing a game. This stage reinforces correct spelling and helps students retain key words before the lesson ends
Title: Context

Sections:

Section: Stage 1: Setting the Scene
Details:
  • Say to the TA: “Do you want to have a classroom party? OK! What do we need?”
  • TA says, “We need food… and classroom presents! You can write a list - let’s write a list together!”
  • Say to Ss: “Do you want to write a classroom party list? Let’s think about our favourite foods and things for school!”
Title: Heads up: Teacher presents the language or activity

Sections:

Section: Focus
Details:
  • Draw Ss attention to the CPT.
  • Ask them what they can see.
Section: Presentation
Details:
  • Ask Ss to read the words in the box.
  • Use the CPT to zoom in to focus on parts of the picture.
  • Ask Ss to look carefully at one area of the picture.
Section: Demonstration
Details:
  • Demonstrate the activity with a co-teacher/student.
Title: Heads down

Sections:

Section: Instruction
Details:
  • Ask Ss to take books out of their bags [Student Book 2A page 19].
  • Ask Ss to open books. Countdown from 5 to keep time.
  • Use gestures. Say: “Find the eight hidden pictures”.
ICQs
Details:
  • Ask: “Are we going to write neatly?” (yes)
  • Ask: “How many pictures are we going to find?” (8)
Section: Activity and Monitor
Details:
  • Monitor. Provide support/stretch if needed.
Section: Feedback
Details:
  • Review answers, correct mistakes together.

Topic: Activity B: Listen and match
20
Title: Stage aims
Summary: To reinforce vocabulary recognition by having students listen and choose words to the correct pictures of classroom and food-related vocabulary. This stage helps check understanding and prepares students for using the vocabulary in speaking activities
Title: Context

Sections:

Section: Stage 2: Checking What We Have
Details:
  • Say to TA: “How many things are on your list?”
  • Ask and answer with TA: “What food do you have? And what classroom things do you have?”
  • Find a match with your TA, e.g. “Hey look! You have a blue marker pen, and I have pizza! It’s a match!”
  • Ask students: “What do you have? Tell your partner, see if you can find a match!”
Title: Heads up

Sections:

Section: Focus
Details:
  • Draw Ss attention to the CPT.
  • Ask Ss what they can see in the activity.
Section: Presentation
Details:
  • Ask them to guess which object will match to each child.
  • Make a note of it on the board. Ss will listen to confirm predictions.
Section: Demonstration
Details:
  • Demonstrate the activity with a co-teacher/student.
Title: Heads down

Sections:

Section: Instruction
Details:
  • Ask Ss to open books. [Student Book 2A page 19].
  • Countdown from 5 to keep time.
  • Use gestures. Say: “Listen and match.”
ICQs
Details:
  • Ask: “Do we speak when we listen?” (no)
  • Ask: “Do we write or draw a line?” (draw a line)
Section: Activity and Monitor
Details:
  • Play the audio. Pause if necessary. Play again.
  • Monitor. Provide support/stretch if needed.
Section: Feedback
Details:
  • Review answers, correct mistakes together.

Topic: Activity Book: Activity A and B
20
Title: Stage aims
Summary: To reinforce word recognition and comprehension by having students read the words, match them to the pictures and follow the path. This stage helps students connect vocabulary with meaning in a fun and interactive way.
Title: Homework correction
Highlights:
  • Have Ss open to the previous lesson’s homework.
  • Have the Ss pair-share.
  • Review answers, correct mistakes together.
Title: Context

Sections:

Section: Stage 3: Finding the Missing body
Details:
  • Say to TA: “Let’s write our list on the board” (Make sure to leave one or two things off the board list)
  • Say to students: “OK that’s a lot of food and things for the classroom! But is that all of them?”
  • Say to TA: “Let’s read our list to the class. Class, listen and tell me … is that all of them?”
  • “Oh thank you! Let’s write those on the board. Well done!”
Title: Heads down: Activity A: Follow the path

Sections:

Section: Instruction
Details:
  • Ask Ss to open books. [Student Book 2A page 73] Countdown from 5 to keep time.
  • Use gestures. Say: “Follow the path.”
ICQs
Details:
  • Ask: “How many words will we follow?” (6)
  • Ask: “Do we draw neatly?” (yes)
Section: Activity and Monitor
Details:
  • Monitor. Provide support/stretch if needed.
Section: Feedback
Details:
  • Have the Ss pair-share.
  • Review answers, correct mistakes together.
  • Mark the Ss’ books with a pen.
Title: Heads down: Activity B: Look and tick.

Sections:

Section: Instruction
Details:
  • Use gestures. Say: “Look and tick.”
ICQs
Details:
  • Ask: “How many questions are there?” (4)
  • Ask: “Do we help each other?” (yes)
Section: Activity and Monitor
Details:
  • Monitor. Provide support/stretch if needed.
Section: Feedback
Details:
  • Have the Ss pair-share.
  • Review answers, correct mistakes together.
  • Mark the Ss’ books with a pen.
Title: Homework setting
Highlights:
  • Complete question 1 of activity C with the Ss.
  • Check errors. Check understanding.
  • Have the Ss out their books and pencils away.
Title: Reward moment
Highlights:
  • Centre yourself, settle the class.
  • Check behaviour expectations.
  • Reward/remind and award points.
Topic: Speaking Task: Board Game
25
Title: Stage aims
Summary: To encourage spoken language practice by having students ask and answer about the classroom and food objects in a picture. This stage helps students build confidence in asking and answering questions naturally while reinforcing key structures in a fun, interactive way.
Title: Context

Sections:

Section: Stage 4: Closing
Details:
  • Say to the students: “Well done! Now we have our lists!”
  • Say to TA: “Let’s read the lists on the board together.”
  • “What’s your favourite? My favourite is … “
  • Say to the students: “You did a great job today — I hope we can have a classroom party soon!! Do you want to play a game to practise what we’ve learned?”
Title: Heads up

Sections:

Section: Focus
Details:
  • Demonstrate tossing a coin and matching one side to yellow and one side to red.
Section: Presentation
Details:
  • Point to the pizza (remind Ss it’s on a yellow plate)
  • Ask: “What have you got?” (I’ve got a pizza)
  • Point to the juice (remind Ss it’s on a red plate)
  • Ask: “What do you want?” (I want juice)
Section: Demonstration
Details:
  • Demonstrate the activity with a co-teacher/student.
Title: Heads down

Sections:

Section: Instruction
Details:
  • Ask Ss to open their books to page [Student Book 2A Page 20].
  • Give Ss a coin or red/yellow token.
  • Use gestures. Say: “Toss a coin”, then “Ask and answer”.
ICQs
Details:
  • Ask: “Are you going to speak English?” (yes)
  • Ask: “Are you going to take turns to speak?” (yes)
Section: Activity and Monitor
Details:
  • Put Ss in pairs.
  • Make sure the Ss have a reference of the words on the CPT if they need it.
  • Monitor. Provide support/stretch if needed.
Section: Feedback
Details:
  • Review language, correct mistakes together.
