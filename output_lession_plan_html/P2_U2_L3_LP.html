    • Activity A: Listen. Then act it out.
    15
    404
    Stage aims
    To help students practice “What do you want?”, “I want…”, and food-related vocabulary by listening to a story and acting it out. This stage reinforces comprehension and speaking skills in an engaging and interactive way.
    Context
    Stage 1: Setting the Scene
    • Say to students: “It’s a sunny day, and Nick and Amy are hungry!”
    • Say to students: ”Do you want to see what food they want?”
    • Pause and reveal the problem: “Can you guess what food is in the cafeteria?”
    • Build anticipation: “Let’s listen to their story and find out what happens!”
    Heads up: Teacher presents the language or activity
    Focus
    • Draw Ss attention to the CPT.
    • Use the CPT tools to reveal the story panels.
    • Encourage Ss to guess what’s in the bag and box.
    • Say: “Let’s read.”
    Presentation
    Skimming
    • Ask: “Are they in the classroom or the cafeteria?” (cafeteria)
    Scanning
    • Ask: “Can you see Rocky?” (Yes)
    Comprehension
    • Ask: “Look at picture 1. Who is hungry?” (Amy/the girl)
    • Mime and ask: “What do you drink?” (juice)
    • Mine and ask: “What do you eat with your fingers?!” (chicken/ pizza)
    Demonstration
    • Demonstrate the activities with a co-teacher/student.
    Heads down
    Instruction
    • Ask Ss to take books out of their bags.
    • Ask Ss to open books [Student Book 2A Page 17]. Countdown from 5 to keep time.
    • Use gestures. Say “Listen, then act it out”.
    ICQs
    • Ask: “Are we going to listen nicely?” (yes)
    • Ask: “Are we going to speak or write?” (speak)
    Activity and Monitor
    • Play the audio. Pause if necessary. Play again.
    • Monitor. Provide support/stretch if needed.
    Feedback
    • Review answers, correct mistakes together.

    Stage aims
    To develop students’ pronunciation and fluency through fun tongue twisters, focusing on clear articulation of sounds like /s/ and /z/. This stage helps improve speech clarity and confidence in speaking.
    Context
    Stage 5: Wrapping Up & Reflection
    • Celebrate: “Wow, that was fantastic! You made some brilliant stories!”
    • Reflect: “Let’s take a moment to think about everything we did today.”
    • Ask: “What was your favourite food? Does Camo have a favourite food?!”
    • “Now let’s have some fun with tongue twisters!”
    Heads up: Teacher presents the language or activity
    Focus
    • Draw Ss attention to the CPT.
    • Say, let’s read.
    Presentation
    Meaning
    • Ask questions about the 2 pictures, drawing attention to the seeds and socks and the zips on the zebras.
    Form
    • Focus on the 4 ‘s’s and 4 ‘z’s. Show the shape of the mouth.
    Pronunciation
    • Drill sentences (class, groups, individual).
    CCQs
    • Mime: ‘s’, Ss guess (They say ‘s’)
    • Mime: ‘z’, Ss guess (They say ‘z’)
    Demonstration
    • Demonstrate the activity with a co-teacher/student.
