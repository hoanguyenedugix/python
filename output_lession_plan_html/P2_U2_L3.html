Topic: Activity A: Listen. Then act it out.
15
Title: Stage aims
Summary: To help students practice “What do you want?”, “I want…”, and food-related vocabulary by listening to a story and acting it out. This stage reinforces comprehension and speaking skills in an engaging and interactive way.
Title: Context

Sections:

Section: Stage 1: Setting the Scene
Details:
  • Say to students: “It’s a sunny day, and Nick and Amy are hungry!”
  • Say to students: ”Do you want to see what food they want?”
  • Pause and reveal the problem: “Can you guess what food is in the cafeteria?”
  • Build anticipation: “Let’s listen to their story and find out what happens!”
Title: Heads up: Teacher presents the language or activity

Sections:

Section: Focus
Details:
  • Draw Ss attention to the CPT.
  • Use the CPT tools to reveal the story panels.
  • Encourage Ss to guess what’s in the bag and box.
  • Say: “Let’s read.”
Section: Presentation
Skimming
Details:
  • Ask: “Are they in the classroom or the cafeteria?” (cafeteria)
Scanning
Details:
  • Ask: “Can you see Rocky?” (Yes)
Comprehension
Details:
  • Ask: “Look at picture 1. Who is hungry?” (Amy/the girl)
  • Mime and ask: “What do you drink?” (juice)
  • Mine and ask: “What do you eat with your fingers?!” (chicken/ pizza)
Section: Demonstration
Details:
  • Demonstrate the activities with a co-teacher/student.
Title: Heads down

Sections:

Section: Instruction
Details:
  • Ask Ss to take books out of their bags.
  • Ask Ss to open books [Student Book 2A Page 17]. Countdown from 5 to keep time.
  • Use gestures. Say “Listen, then act it out”.
ICQs
Details:
  • Ask: “Are we going to listen nicely?” (yes)
  • Ask: “Are we going to speak or write?” (speak)
Section: Activity and Monitor
Details:
  • Play the audio. Pause if necessary. Play again.
  • Monitor. Provide support/stretch if needed.
Section: Feedback
Details:
  • Review answers, correct mistakes together.

Topic: Activity B: Look and choose
20
Title: Stage aims
Summary: To develop students’ reading comprehension and vocabulary recognition by matching characters (Amy and Cafeteria Lady) to a picture using information from the story. This stage reinforces understanding of “want/have got” and food-related vocabulary in context before moving on to speaking or writing activities.
Title: Context

Sections:

Section: Stage 2: Identifying What They Have
Details:
  • React enthusiastically: “You all acted out the story so well! You are great!”

  • Say to the students: “Hmm… I can’t remember… Is it pizza? Fish? No? What do you think?”
  • “OK! Let’s look and see!”

Topic: Activity Book: Activity A
15
Title: Stage aims
Summary: To develop students’ reading comprehension and sequencing skills by reading a new story in the activity book and numbering events in the correct order. This stage helps students understand story structure and reinforces the use of “want/have got” and food-related vocabulary.
Title: Homework correction
Highlights:
  • Have Ss open to the previous lesson’s homework.
  • Have the Ss pair-share.
  • Review answers, correct mistakes together.
Title: Context

Sections:

Section: Stage 3: Changing the Story
Details:
  • Praise their effort: “Great work! Now we know what food Nick and Amy want.”
  • Introduce the next challenge: “But, what about these two children…”
  • Say: “Do you want to read a new story with some new food?”
  • Build curiosity: “Do you think they want chicken? Or maybe they want soup and pizza? Let’s see!”
Title: Heads down

Sections:

Section: Instruction
Details:
  • Ask Ss to take books [Student Book 2A page 71] and pencils out of their bags.
  • Ask Ss to open books. Countdown from 5 to keep time.
  • Use gestures. Say: “Read and number.”
ICQs
Details:
  • Ask: “Do we write neatly?” (yes)
  • Ask: “Do we help each other?” (yes)
Section: Activity and Monitor
Details:
  • Monitor. Provide support/stretch if needed.
Section: Feedback
Details:
  • Have the Ss pair-share.
  • Review answers, correct mistakes together.
  • Mark the Ss’ books with a pen.
Title: Homework setting
Highlights:
  • Complete question 1 of activity B with the Ss.
  • Complete question 1 of activity C with the Ss.
  • Check errors. Check understanding.
  • Have the Ss put their books and pencils away.
Title: Reward moment
Highlights:
  • Centre yourself, settle the class.
  • Check behaviour expectations.
  • Reward/remind and award points.

Topic: Tongue Twisters
20
Title: Stage aims
Summary: To develop students’ pronunciation and fluency through fun tongue twisters, focusing on clear articulation of sounds like /s/ and /z/. This stage helps improve speech clarity and confidence in speaking.
Title: Context

Sections:

Section: Stage 5: Wrapping Up & Reflection
Details:
  • Celebrate: “Wow, that was fantastic! You made some brilliant stories!”
  • Reflect: “Let’s take a moment to think about everything we did today.”
  • Ask: “What was your favourite food? Does Camo have a favourite food?!”
  • “Now let’s have some fun with tongue twisters!”
Title: Heads up: Teacher presents the language or activity

Sections:

Section: Focus
Details:
  • Draw Ss attention to the CPT.
  • Say, let’s read.
Section: Presentation
Meaning
Details:
  • Ask questions about the 2 pictures, drawing attention to the seeds and socks and the zips on the zebras.
Form
Details:
  • Focus on the 4 ‘s’s and 4 ‘z’s. Show the shape of the mouth.
Pronunciation
Details:
  • Drill sentences (class, groups, individual).
CCQs
Details:
  • Mime: ‘s’, Ss guess (They say ‘s’)
  • Mime: ‘z’, Ss guess (They say ‘z’)
Section: Demonstration
Details:
  • Demonstrate the activity with a co-teacher/student.
