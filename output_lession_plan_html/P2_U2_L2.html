Topic: Activity A: Listen and say. Then practice.
15
Title: Stage aims
Summary: To introduce and practice using new food vocabulary (pizza, fish, chicken, steak, soup, juice) with asking and answering about what people want using the question word “what” through a listen and say activity, followed by practice. This stage helps students understand and use the target grammar before applying it in speaking tasks.
Title: Context

Sections:

Section: Stage 1: Setting the Scene
Details:
  • In preparation: stick food vocab FCs on to the whiteboard.
  • Ask the TA/Ss: “Do you want to come to my cafeteria? OK! Let’s go…”
  • Mime marching around the classroom and opening a door...
  • Say to TA/Ss: “Welcome! This is my cafeteria! Do you like it?”
  • Say to TA/Ss, “And this is the food. What do you want?”
  • TA asks: “I want a (food item)”
  • Say to TA: “OK! You want a (food item). No problem! Here you go.”
  • TA asks: “‘And… I want a (food item) and a (food item) and a (food item)”
  • Say to Ss: “Wow! That’s a lot of food. What does he/she want?!”
  • Say to Ss: “Well done! Let’s practise”

Meaning

Pronunciation
Details:

CCQs
Details:
  • Mime and ask: “What is round?” (pizza)
  • Mime and ask: “What do you drink?” (juice)
  • Mine and ask: “What do you eat with your fingers?!” (chicken/ pizza)
Section: Presentation: Grammar 🎧24
Meaning
Details:
  • Give a co-teacher a few flashcards, demonstrate asking and answering: “What do you want” “I want …”
Form
Details:
  • Briefly review not using ‘a/an’, ‘I want pizza’ not ‘I want a pizza’
Pronunciation
Details:
  • Drill sentences (class, groups, individual).
CCQs
Details:
  • Ask: “What does you want? Or, what do you want?” (do you want)
  • Ask: “I want pizza? Or, I want a pizza” (I want pizza)
Section: Demonstration
Details:
  • Demonstrate the activities with a co-teacher/student.

Section: Stage 2: Getting the Order Ready
Details:
  • Say to the TA: “I need to get my kitchen ready! Can you help me?”

  • Ask the students: “OK, can you tell me what’s on the menu?” Students name as many foods as they can.

Section: Stage 3: Keeping Track of What We Know
Details:

Topic: Speaking Task: Our Class Restaurants
30
Title: Stage aims
Summary: To practice asking and answering questions using “What do you want? What does he/she want?” and acting out a scene in a restaurant. This stage reinforces the target language in a meaningful and interactive way.
Title: Context

Sections:

Section: Stage 4: Spotting the Mistakes
Details:
  • Say to the students: “OK thank you! Now I have my food and my menu. Wow. Let’s review… (review list and hold up FCs at the same time, make one or two mistakes). Is that right?!”
  • Students check until the list is correct.
  • Say to Ss: “Perfect! Now, let’s get ready to eat. What do you want?”
  • Students choose food, TA and Ss help serve it up. Mime eating delicious food together.
  • “Amazing! You are the best! Thank you for helping me in my cafeteria.”
Title: Heads up: Teacher presents the language or activity

Sections:

Section: Focus
Details:
  • Draw Ss attention to the CPT.
  • Demonstrate circling three things you want.
Section: Presentation
Details:
  • Mime welcoming TA to your restaurant, TA sits down at a table.
  • Teacher acts as a server and asks the question. TA acts as a Customer and replies. Teacher acts as Chef and asks the Ss what the TA wants. Swap roles.
Section: Demonstration
Details:
  • Demonstrate the activity in a group of three with a student.
Title: Heads down

Sections:

Section: Instruction
Details:
  • Ask Ss to turn to page [Students Book 2A page 16]
  • Use gestures. Say: “Circle three things. Then make a group.”
  • Use gestures. Say: “Choose roles. Then act out a restaurant scene.”
ICQs
Details:
  • Ask: “Who writes on the board?” (Chef)
  • Ask: “Who orders the food?” (Customer)
  • Ask: “Who takes the order?” (Server)
Section: Activity and Monitor
Details:
  • Divide the class into groups. Each group can have several customers.
  • Monitor. Provide support/stretch if needed.
Section: Feedback
Details:
  • Review language, correct mistakes together.

Topic: Song: What do you want?
15
Title: Stage aims
Summary: To reinforce the target language (“What do you want?”) through a fun and repetitive song, helping students build confidence in pronunciation, rhythm, and natural language use in an engaging way.
Title: Context

Sections:

Section: Stage 5: Wrapping Up & Celebration
Details:
  • Say: “Do you want to sing on the way home? Let’s learn a song together!”
  • Pretend to listen to an imaginary tune. “It’s a fun song about food. Let’s practice!”
  • Together with the TA, sing the song and encourage Ss to join in: “Well done, everyone!”
  • Give out praise: “You all did a fantastic job!”
