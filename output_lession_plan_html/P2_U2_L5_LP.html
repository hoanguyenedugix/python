    • Activity A: Listen and read.
    20
    418
    Stage aims
    To develop reading comprehension and listening skills through a LETS-based activity on Science. Students will listen and read about useful plastic body, linking language learning with real-world knowledge about materials.
    Context
    Stage 1: Setting the Scene
    • Say to the Ss: “We have lots of things that come from something different! The window is made of glass, the door is made of wood from a tree… But what about these classroom things?”
    • Say to the TA: “Do you know what this (pick up various classroom body) is made of?”
    • TA says: “I think so… but I want to learn more!”
    • Say to students: “Do you want to learn more about these things? OK let’s go!”
    Heads up: Teacher presents the language or activity
    Focus
    • Draw Ss attention to the CPT.
    • Say, let’s read.
    Presentation
    Skimming
    • Ask: “What are classroom objects made of?” (plastic)
    Scanning
    • Say: “Underline all the classroom objects.”
    Comprehension
    • Mime and Ask: “Is plastic light or heavy?” (light)
    • Ask: “How many classroom objects are there?”
    Demonstration
    • Demonstrate the activity with a co-teacher/student.
    Heads down
    Instruction
    • Ask Ss to take books out of their bags [Student Book 2A page 21]
    • Ask Ss to open books. Countdown from 5 to keep time.
    • Use gestures. Say: “Listen and read.”
    ICQs
    • Ask: “Are we going to listen or speak?” (listen)
    Activity and Monitor
    • Play the audio. Pause if necessary.
    • Play again. Ss read aloud quietly.
    • Monitor. Provide support/stretch if needed.
    Feedback
    • Review reading, correct mistakes together.

    Stage aims
    To develop phonics recognition, reading comprehension, coloring to reinforce sound recognition, and spelling skills by having students find and colour words with /sh/. This stage helps reinforce sound differentiation and word meaning in a structured and interactive way.
    Context
    Stage 3: Sorting the Sounds
    • Say to the TA (or TA to Teacher): “Let’s try colouring some words! Are you ready?”
    • (Write a number of words from the vocabulary list or above level on the board)
    • Say to TA: “You have 5 seconds! Colour the … sh words!” (Ss countdown and see how many TA can colour in the time)
    Heads up
    Focus
    • Draw Ss attention to the CPT.
    Presentation
    • Say each word and nod your head when there is a sh sound. Shake your head if there is no sh sound.
    • Underline she and fish. Ask theTA/Ss which word they think it is.
    • Underline chicken and chips. Ask the TA/Ss which word they think it is.
    Demonstration
    • Demonstrate the activity with a co-teacher/student.
    Heads down: A: Find and colour sh words.
    Instruction
    • Ask Ss to get their pencils and erasers.
    • Ask Ss to open books [Student Book 2A page 112]. Countdown from 5 to keep time.
    • Use gestures. Say: “Find and colour sh words.”
    ICQs
    • Ask: “How many sh words are there?” (5)
    Activity and Monitor
    • Monitor. Provide support/stretch if needed.
    Feedback
    • Review answers, correct mistakes together.

    15

    Stage aims
    To develop phonics recognition and spelling skills by having students look and match words to pictures, then write and colour in a picture. This stage reinforces understanding of the /sh/ and /ch/ sounds in a structured way.
    Context
    Stage 2: Noticing the shape of words
    • Say to the TA: “Let’s play a game… you write a word shape and I’ll write a word to fit inside, what do you think?” (TA draws one or two 4 and 5-letter word shapes that match the words on p.113)
    • Say to the TA: “Hmmm OK, I think I know!” (write 4 and 5-letter words the students will know)
    • TA says: “Well done! Now it’s your turn!”
    • Say to TA: “OK… here you go!” (draw one or two 4 and 5-letter word shapes)
    Heads up
    Focus
    • Draw Ss attention to the CPT.
    Presentation
    • Ask Ss what they can see.
    Demonstration
    • Demonstrate the activity with a co-teacher/student.
    Heads down: A: Look and read.
    Instruction
    • Ask Ss to get their pencils and erasers.
    • Ask Ss to open books. [Student Book 2A page 113]. Countdown from 5 to keep time.
    • Use gestures. Say: “Look and read.”
    ICQs
    • Ask: “How many pictures are there?” (2)
    Activity and Monitor
    • Monitor. Provide support/stretch if needed.
    Feedback
    • Review answers, correct mistakes together.
    Heads down: B: Write.
    Instruction
    • Have Ss write the words first, focusing on penmanship.
    • After writing, have the Ss do finger blending.
    • Show the number of letters by holding up fingers.
    • Then put fingers together for sounds with 2 letters.
    • Point at each sound and blend the word: f-i-sh, fish.
    • Use gestures. Say: “Write.”
    ICQs
    • Ask: “Are you going to write neatly?” (yes)
    Activity and Monitor
    • Monitor. Provide support/stretch if needed.
    Feedback
    • Review answers, correct mistakes together.
    Heads down: C: Colour.
    Instruction
    • Have Ss read the sentences.
    • Use gestures. Say: “Colour.”
    ICQs
    • Ask: “Are you going to colour neatly?” (yes)
    Activity and Monitor
    • Monitor. Provide support/stretch if needed.
    Feedback
    • Review answers, correct mistakes together.
