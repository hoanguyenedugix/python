Lesson Outcomes

Students can listen to a story and identify key characters and jobs mentioned.

Communication

Support Strategies:

  • (Listening) Say: “Listen for doctor, firefighter, police officer.” Students raise hands or clap when they hear those jobs during the story.

Stretch Strategies:

  • Alternative Ending Skit – High flyers write and act out a new ending for the story that includes 2–3 target jobs and the “He/She is a...” or “They’re...” structure. E.g., Then the doctor arrived and helped the dancer.

Students can read the story aloud, act it out, and answer questions using the target language.

Communication

Support Strategies:

  • (Speaking) Read the story aloud. Students echo each line. Assign small groups to act out the roles using simplified lines.

Stretch Strategies:

  • Alternative Ending Skit – High flyers write and act out a new ending for the story that includes 2–3 target jobs and the “He/She is a...” or “They’re...” structure. E.g., Then the doctor arrived and helped the dancer.

Students can create a simple, imaginative ending to the story by drawing or acting it out.

Future Skills

Support Strategies:

  • (4Cs – Creativity) Pause the story before the ending. Ask: “What could happen next?” Students act out or draw their ideas.

Stretch Strategies:

  • Alternative Ending Skit – High flyers write and act out a new ending for the story that includes 2–3 target jobs and the “He/She is a...” or “They’re...” structure. E.g., Then the doctor arrived and helped the dancer.

Language Transfer Analysis

1. Consonant clusters:

  • Consonant clusters do not exist in Vietnamese words, so they need special attention. Remember that clusters don't come only at the beginning of words – end clusters can be even harder (dogs, task, crisp, crisps, snacks)

2. Released final consonants:

  • In Vietnamese, final consonants are 'held in' – you don't actually hear them released. But in English, the final consonant of cat, top, rock, and so on are released with a puff of air. Make sure you are aware of this difference.

3. Contractions:

  • Do not exist in standard Vietnamese, written or spoken. The hardest part for YLs is learning to pronounce them naturally: there's a dog rather than there is a dog.
