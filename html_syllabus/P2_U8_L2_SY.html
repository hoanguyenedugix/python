Lesson Outcomes

Students can recognise and name six rooms (bedroom, kitchen, living room, bathroom, attic, garage) when listening and looking at visual prompts.

Communication

Support Strategies:

  • (Vocab) Use SB visuals of rooms. Say: “This is the bathroom.” Students repeat, then close books. Teacher says a room, students mime actions done there.

Stretch Strategies:

  • “Design Your Dream House” – Logical Room-Item Match
  • Give each group 6 room names on small paper cards. Groups draw one flashcard at a time (e.g., cupboard, mirror), and must decide as a team which room it goes in and make a sentence:
  • “There’s a cupboard in the kitchen.”
  • Add a twist: some flashcards are "wild" (can go in more than one room) and must be justified.

Students can say simple statements about items in rooms using “There are (number) (items) in the (room).” and ask and answer questions using “Are there (number) (items) in the (room)?” with responses like “Yes, there are.” / “No, there aren’t.”

Communication

Support Strategies:

  • (Speaking) Write: “There are ___ ___ in the ___.” on board. Model with: “There are two chairs in the kitchen.” Students ask/answer using room/object cards.

Stretch Strategies:

  • “Design Your Dream House” – Logical Room-Item Match
  • Give each group 6 room names on small paper cards. Groups draw one flashcard at a time (e.g., cupboard, mirror), and must decide as a team which room it goes in and make a sentence:
  • “There’s a cupboard in the kitchen.”
  • Add a twist: some flashcards are "wild" (can go in more than one room) and must be justified.

Students can use the target structure “There are ___ ___ in the ___.” and ask and answer using “Are there ___ ___ in the ___?” / “Yes, there are.” / “No, there aren’t.” to talk about items in rooms.

Communication

Support Strategies:

Language Transfer Analysis

1. Plural nouns:

  • Hai con chó >> two dog [two dogs]. Not only the form, but the pronunciation is tough here, as it changes depending on the final sound of the noun [s,z,iz]

2. Released final consonants:

  • In Vietnamese, final consonants are 'held in' – you don't actually hear them released. But in English, the final consonant of cat, top, rock, and so on are released with a puff of air. Make sure you are aware of this difference.

3. There is vs it is:

  • Often confused – Kindergarteners will tend to prefer 'it is' for everything, even though the Vietnamese is very different for the two structures

4. Plural forms of it is and there is:

  • Plural forms of these structures do not exist in Vietnamese. It's difficult for kids to remember to produce them, particularly because the plural element comes before the noun phrase. There are two houses
