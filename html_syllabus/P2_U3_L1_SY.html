Lesson Outcomes

Students can recognise and name six animals (duck, horse, turkey, cow, goat, rabbit) when listening and looking at visual prompts.

Communication

Support Strategies:

  • (Vocab) Show flashcards of animals (e.g., duck, goat). Say the name. Students repeat and match them to pictures in SB.

Stretch Strategies:

  • Guess Who?" Animal Riddles – Students write and present short riddles about farm animals using the structure “It’s a…” / “They’re…” and descriptive clues (e.g., “It gives us milk. It says ‘moo.’ What is it?”).

Students can ask and answer simple questions about animals using "What's this?" / "What are these?" and respond with "It's a..." / "They're...".

Communication

Support Strategies:

  • (Speaking) Point to an animal and ask: “What’s this?” Students answer: “It’s a goat.” Repeat with pair work using SB or flashcards.

Stretch Strategies:

  • Guess Who?" Animal Riddles – Students write and present short riddles about farm animals using the structure “It’s a…” / “They’re…” and descriptive clues (e.g., “It gives us milk. It says ‘moo.’ What is it?”).

Students can use the target structure “What's this?” / “What are these?” / “It’s a ___.” / “They’re ___.” to talk about animals.

Communication

Support Strategies:

  • (Grammar) Write Q&A on the board: “What’s this?” – “It’s a ___.” Then “What are these?” – “They’re ___.” Students practise with toys or images.

Language Transfer Analysis

1. Consonant clusters:

  • Consonant clusters do not exist in Vietnamese words, so they need special attention. Remember that clusters don't come only at the beginning of words – end clusters can be even harder (dogs, task, crisp, crisps, snacks)

2. Released final consonants:

  • In Vietnamese, final consonants are 'held in' – you don't actually hear them released. But in English, the final consonant of cat, top, rock, and so on are released with a puff of air. Make sure you are aware of this difference.

3. Contractions:

  • Do not exist in standard Vietnamese, written or spoken. The hardest part for YLs is learning to pronounce them naturally: there's a dog rather than there is a dog.

4. Plural forms of it is and there is:

  • Plural forms of these structures do not exist in Vietnamese. It's difficult for kids to remember to produce them, particularly because the plural element comes before the noun phrase. There are two houses
