Lesson Outcomes

Students can recognise and name six instruments (guitar, flute, violin, cello, piano, drums) when listening and looking at visual prompts.

Communication

Support Strategies:

  • (Vocab) Show instrument flashcards or SB visuals. Say: “This is a violin.” Students repeat and match or mime playing the instrument.

Stretch Strategies:

  • Grammar Swap Game
  • After practicing “Do you play…?” and “Does he/she play…?”, challenge students to swap roles and form new questions using different pronouns (e.g., “Do they play the piano?” / “Yes, they do.”). Encourage them to create mini conversations in pairs that include two or more instruments.

Students can ask and answer simple questions about playing instruments using “Do you play the...?” / “Does he/she play the...?” and respond with “Yes, I do.” / “No, I don’t.” or “Yes, he/she does.” / “No, he/she doesn’t.”

Communication

Support Strategies:

  • (Speaking) Ask: “Do you play the piano?” Students respond “Yes, I do.” or “No, I don’t.” Practise in pairs using flashcards or gestures.

Stretch Strategies:

  • Grammar Swap Game
  • After practicing “Do you play…?” and “Does he/she play…?”, challenge students to swap roles and form new questions using different pronouns (e.g., “Do they play the piano?” / “Yes, they do.”). Encourage them to create mini conversations in pairs that include two or more instruments.

Students can use the target structure “Do you play the ___?” / “Yes, I do.” / “No, I don’t.” and “Does he/she play the ___?” / “Yes, he/she does.” / “No, he/she doesn’t.” to talk about instruments.

Communication

Support Strategies:

  • (Grammar) Write model questions/answers: “Do you play the ___?” / “Does she play the ___?” Students take turns asking and answering with partners.

Stretch Strategies:

  • Grammar Swap Game
  • After practicing “Do you play…?” and “Does he/she play…?”, challenge students to swap roles and form new questions using different pronouns (e.g., “Do they play the piano?” / “Yes, they do.”). Encourage them to create mini conversations in pairs that include two or more instruments.

Language Transfer Analysis

1. Question formation with do-support:

  • Bạn có thích ăn bánh mì không? >> you like eat bread? [do you like to eat bánh mì?]

2. Negatives with do-support:

  • Con không thích >> I no/not like [I don't like]

3. Sport and play:

  • Playground words tend to be long and phonetically difficult (slide, see-saw, swings, climbing frame)
  • Some sports not well known in VN (baseball, cricket, rugby, paragliding, water skiing)

4. Hobbies and activities:

  • A wide variety of syllabus hobbies may be less popular or available to some Vietnamese students (practising the flute; seeing famous paintings), although many overlap

5. Unstressed vowels (schwa):

  • Unstressed vowels do not really exist in Vietnamese, so these need extra practice. Make sure the final vowel of computer is a light schwa, not a heavy com-pu-TER. Same with the final vowel of banana. Or the second syllable of elephant. And so on.

6. Inversion in questions:

  • Bạn muốn ăn gì? >> you want to eat what? [what do you want to eat?]
  • This is extremely difficult to master, especially combined with do-support.
