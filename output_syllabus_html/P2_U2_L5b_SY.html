Lesson Outcomes

Students can ask and answer questions using “What...?” (e.g., “What do you want?” / “What does he/she want?”).

Communication

Support Strategies:

  • (Grammar) Write jumbled questions on the board (e.g. “want / what / do / you?”). Students unscramble, then write and read aloud the correct question.

Stretch Strategies:

  • Mixed-Up Sentence Fix: Write jumbled “What…?” questions on the board (e.g., “got you what have?”). Students work in pairs to fix and read aloud the correct sentence. Challenge them to write their own jumbled versions for peers.

Students can recognise and identify the sounds “sh” and “ch”.

Communication

Support Strategies:

  • (Phonics) Say /sh/ and /ch/ sounds. Ask: “Which sound?” Students hold up 1 finger for /sh/ and 2 fingers for /ch/ as you say sample words.

Stretch Strategies:

  • Mixed-Up Sentence Fix: Write jumbled “What…?” questions on the board (e.g., “got you what have?”). Students work in pairs to fix and read aloud the correct sentence. Challenge them to write their own jumbled versions for peers.

Students can complete short sentence frames and unscramble word order to form correct “What...?” questions.

Communication

Support Strategies:

  • (Writing) Display a sentence frame: “What do you want? – I want ___.” Students complete using their own food choice, then ask/answer in pairs.

Stretch Strategies:

  • Mixed-Up Sentence Fix: Write jumbled “What…?” questions on the board (e.g., “got you what have?”). Students work in pairs to fix and read aloud the correct sentence. Challenge them to write their own jumbled versions for peers.

Language Transfer Analysis

1. Silent letters:

  • Systematic kinds of silent letters, such as 'magic e' in late or mote, or silent gh in thought and bought, are entirely alien to Vietnamese. They are often acquired as sight words first; the teacher can explain at higher levels.

2. Released final consonants:

  • In Vietnamese, final consonants are 'held in' – you don't actually hear them released. But in English, the final consonant of cat, top, rock, and so on are released with a puff of air. Make sure you are aware of this difference.
