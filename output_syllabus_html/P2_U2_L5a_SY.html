Lesson Outcomes

Students can read a short text about useful things made of plastic and find key details such as classroom items and their descriptions.

Communication

Support Strategies:

  • (Reading) Read text about plastic. Ask: “What is made of plastic?” “Is a glue stick plastic?” Students highlight key words in SB.

Stretch Strategies:

  • Sound Hunt: Say a sound (e.g., /ʃ/ or /tʃ/), and have students search the classroom for flashcards or items with matching sounds (real or imagined). Students then say or write words using that sound.

Students can trace and write the letter combinations “sh” and “ch” to form simple words.

Communication

Support Strategies:

  • (Phonics) Say and write “sh” and “ch” words: “ship,” “chip,” “chair,” “shop.” Students trace the letters and sort them by sound.

Stretch Strategies:

  • Sound Hunt: Say a sound (e.g., /ʃ/ or /tʃ/), and have students search the classroom for flashcards or items with matching sounds (real or imagined). Students then say or write words using that sound.

Students can say one thing they know about plastic and list things that are made from plastic.

Real world learning

Support Strategies:

  • (Science) Show 2 objects: one plastic, one not. Ask: “Which is plastic?” Students guess, touch, and say why (e.g. “It’s light/soft”).

Stretch Strategies:

  • Sound Hunt: Say a sound (e.g., /ʃ/ or /tʃ/), and have students search the classroom for flashcards or items with matching sounds (real or imagined). Students then say or write words using that sound.

Language Transfer Analysis

1. Silent letters:

  • Systematic kinds of silent letters, such as 'magic e' in late or mote, or silent gh in thought and bought, are entirely alien to Vietnamese. They are often acquired as sight words first; the teacher can explain at higher levels.

2. Released final consonants:

  • In Vietnamese, final consonants are 'held in' – you don't actually hear them released. But in English, the final consonant of cat, top, rock, and so on are released with a puff of air. Make sure you are aware of this difference.
