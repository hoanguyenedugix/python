Lesson Outcomes

Students can write short words and sentences (e.g., “Have you got a scooter?”) clearly and accurately.

Communication

Support Strategies:

  • (Writing) Model: “Have you got a scooter?” on the board. Students copy carefully, focusing on spacing and punctuation.

Stretch Strategies:

  • ❝Write and Find Partner❞ – Students draw a toy and write a sentence: “Have you got a ___?” They walk around the room reading their question and answering classmates with “Yes, I have.” / “No, I haven’t.” They sit when they find a match.

Students can listen to spoken descriptions and assign the correct toy to the correct person.

Communication

Support Strategies:

  • (Listening) Say: “He’s got a skateboard.” or “She hasn’t got a bat.” Students choose the correct toy/person from the SB.

Stretch Strategies:

Students can work together politely and take turns to play a collaborative game.

Language Transfer Analysis

1. Food:

  • Food in syllabi typically Western-centric;
  • banh mi >> bread

2. Holidays and travel:

  • Certain common holiday activities are less likely to overlap with those of Western kids (eating seafood at the beach, going on tours in large groups)

3. Contractions:

  • Do not exist in standard Vietnamese, written or spoken. The hardest part for YLs is learning to pronounce them naturally: there's a dog rather than there is a dog.

4. Released final consonants:

  • In Vietnamese, final consonants are 'held in' – you don't actually hear them released. But in English, the final consonant of cat, top, rock, and so on are released with a puff of air. Make sure you are aware of this difference.
