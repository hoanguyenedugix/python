Lesson Outcomes

Students can read a short text about daily routines in Spain and find key details such as times and activities.

Communication

Support Strategies:

  • (Reading) Read text about a Spanish routine. Ask: “When does he sleep?” Students underline times and daily activities in the SB.

Stretch Strategies:

  • After reading about Spain’s siesta, students write a short comparison paragraph starting with a scaffold: “In Spain, people... In Vietnam, we usually... I think this is because...”

Students can trace and write the letter combinations “ai” and “oa” to form simple words.

Communication

Support Strategies:

  • (Phonics) Say /ai/ and /oa/ sounds (e.g., rain, boat). Students repeat and sort words by sound. Then trace letters in notebooks.

Stretch Strategies:

  • After reading about Spain’s siesta, students write a short comparison paragraph starting with a scaffold: “In Spain, people... In Vietnam, we usually... I think this is because...”

Students can explain that people in Spain rest during the middle of the day and compare their own routine to someone in Spain.

Real world learning

Support Strategies:

Language Transfer Analysis

1. Consonant clusters:

  • Consonant clusters do not exist in Vietnamese words, so they need special attention. Remember that clusters don't come only at the beginning of words – end clusters can be even harder (dogs, task, crisp, crisps, snacks)

2. Hobbies and activities:

  • A wide variety of syllabus hobbies may be less popular or available to some Vietnamese students (practising the flute; seeing famous paintings), although many overlap

3. Released final consonants:

  • In Vietnamese, final consonants are 'held in' – you don't actually hear them released. But in English, the final consonant of cat, top, rock, and so on are released with a puff of air. Make sure you are aware of this difference.

4. Contractions:

  • Do not exist in standard Vietnamese, written or spoken. The hardest part for YLs is learning to pronounce them naturally: there's a dog rather than there is a dog.
